# CSWeb Documentation — Audit Report (2026-06-30)

Audited the live help set: `index.html`, `help.html`, the four guides
(`enumerator`, `hub`, `hcw`, CAPI Manual) and the four crosswalks, plus
`assets/docs.css`. Three lenses: **automated technical** (Lighthouse), **link
integrity**, and a **technical-writer content/standards review** against
Diátaxis · ISO/IEC/IEEE 26514 · Google/Microsoft style · WCAG 2.2.

**Headline:** a strong, professional set. Technical hygiene is near-perfect
(accessibility + best-practices 100 across the board; the task-orientation and
voice are exemplary, especially the CAPI Manual). The real weaknesses are
**consistency and integrity**, not writing: a sync message the field guides word
differently from the actual app, four live-only assets the repo can't reproduce,
non-descriptive alt text on the 7 diagrams, mixed cross-reference notation in the
manual, and a couple of leaked author placeholders / off-by-one counts.

## Findings (by severity)

### HIGH

**H1 · Sync-confirmation wording doesn't match the app** — `enumerator-guide.html`
(§11) and `help.html` (Syncing) tell the enumerator to wait for **"Sync complete /
success."**, but the real on-screen toast (documented + screenshotted in the
manual §XIII) is **"Successfully synced."** An enumerator is watching for a string
the app never shows. *Standard: consistency / terminology.* **Fix:** change both
guides to quote the real message, "Successfully synced."

**H2 · Blank Support Contacts annex while urgent pointers route there** —
`capi-manual.html` §XVII·H ships as an empty `______` template, yet §4.5 (forgot
password), §XV·L (escalation), and §XVI·F (lost/stolen device) all send the reader
there. The single most actionable fact — who to call — is absent everywhere
(every page only says "contact your coordinator/supervisor"). *Standard:
completeness (ISO 26514 §8).* **Fix:** ASPSI-side — fill the table, or state
explicitly that contacts are issued at training and give a central CAPI/IT line.
*(Known ASPSI-pending item per the manual's STATUS.)*

### MEDIUM

**M1 · Source/deploy drift — 4 live-only assets the repo can't reproduce** —
these are **live (HTTP 200)** but **absent from the repo** source tree:
`docs/dashboard.html`, `docs/map.html` (generated on-box by the dashboard/map
gen — expected to be live-only), and `docs/img/01-csentry-terms-of-service.png` +
`docs/img/02-csentry-home-no-apps.png` (referenced by the enumerator guide). They
are **not 404s for end users**, but a fresh deploy from the repo would drop the two
images. *Standard: integrity.* **Fix:** pull the two images down into the repo and
commit; document dashboard/map as generated-on-box (the doc-audit skill already
whitelists them).

**M2 · Non-descriptive `alt="diagram"` on the 7 workflow SVGs** — `capi-manual.html`.
The diagrams ("A normal day," "the life of a case," the §XV troubleshooting
decision tree) carry real information that's lost to screen readers. *Lighthouse
passed A11y 100 because the alt is non-empty — exactly what automated tools miss
and content review catches.* *Standard: WCAG 1.1.1.* **Fix:** add descriptive alt
in the build pipeline (the `build-csweb-help.sh` mermaid→SVG step).

**M3 · Leaked author/production placeholder in the live manual** — §II.A:
*"📷 Screenshot 2.A (optional, add at kit prep) … drop it in here before
printing."* This is an internal build note visible to end users (the HTML-comment
sanitizer stripped comments but not this body-text note). *Standard:
professionalism.* **Fix:** add the photo or remove the placeholder block.

**M4 · Inconsistent cross-reference notation + 2 dangling pointers** —
`capi-manual.html` cites the same subsections three ways (`§XI·D`, `§XI·6`,
`§11.6`); `§XII·G` points to the wrong subsection (the content is in §12.6); and
`§I·D / §1` references a section "1" that doesn't exist (sections are Roman). *Standard:
consistency.* **Fix:** standardize on decimal (`§11.8`) and fix the two dangling refs.

**M5 · Crosswalk "(complete)" badges contradict their own subtitles** —
`help.html` badges the crosswalks "(complete)" while the subtitle says "…still
being captured." Reader can't tell if they're done. **Fix:** drop the badge or
move incomplete items to an explicit "in progress" note.

**M6 · Off-by-one section count** — `help.html` F3 card says "All 13 sections
(A–L)"; A–L is **12**. **Fix:** correct to 12 (F4's "A–Q = 17" is right).

**M7 · "PSGC" never expanded** — `enumerator-guide.html` (and the manual §9.4) use
"PSGC cascade" without spelling out Philippine Standard Geographic Code, for a
field audience. **Fix:** expand on first use per page.

**M8 · Over-long Map Report card** — `help.html`'s Map Report card is a single
~90-word sentence. **Fix:** one plain sentence on the card; move QA-flag detail to
the Map Report page.

**M9 · Procedure duplication (help.html ↔ enumerator-guide)** — help.html restates
the enumerator guide's Collect/Sync procedures; the two copies have **already
drifted** (that's the H1 sync-message mismatch). **Fix:** make help.html a hub that
*links* to procedures; keep one authoritative copy.

**M10 · Feature duplication (hub-guide ↔ manual §XIV)** — the whole hub workflow is
documented fully in both. *Standard: maintainability.* **Fix:** designate one
canonical (the manual) and have the other summarize + deep-link.

**M11 · ~18 `<h1>` elements + TOC `<h2>` above the section `<h1>`s** —
`capi-manual.html` (a side effect of one `<h1>` per section in the build). Inverts
the screen-reader outline. *Standard: WCAG 1.3.1 / heading hierarchy.* **Fix:**
demote section headings to `<h2>`/`<h3>` in `csweb-help-template.html` so there's a
single page `<h1>`.

**M12 · Heading case inconsistent across the set** — manual uses Title Case section
headings; the guides use sentence case (Google/MS style = sentence case). **Fix:**
standardize on sentence case.

### LOW

- **L1 · "Bisaya" vs "Cebuano"** listed as separate dialects (often the same) — `enumerator-guide.html`. Disambiguate or drop the duplicate.
- **L2 · Do/Don't restates one rule** — "keep your login private" / "don't share the tablet login" — `enumerator-guide.html`. Replace one.
- **L3 · "Synchronize" / "Sync" / "sync up"** mixed — standardize on "Sync" in prose, "Synchronize" only for the button.
- **L4 · `help.html` inline `<style>` duplicates `docs.css` and has diverged** (lacks `.banner`/`figure`/table/`.dd-grid`). **Fix:** have help.html load `/assets/docs.css` like the other pages.
- **L5 · Privacy specifics** (NPC PIC-000-358-2021, RA 10173) are in the HCW guide but the manual §XVI is only general — align.
- **L6 · Annex E/F live `<input type="checkbox">`** are inert (state not saved) — add `disabled` or use print glyphs.
- **L7 · GPS caption "Accuracy 19 m, Satellites 0"** is internally inconsistent (the caption does explain 0 = unreported) — annotate as a capture artifact.
- **L8 · `help.html` mode-mix** (hub + how-to + reference) — the root of M9; lean it toward a pure hub + short reference.
- **L9 · No pure tutorial / explanation doc** — the enumerator guide doubles as tutorial, manual §III as explanation. Acceptable at this size; make it a conscious choice if onboarding new enumerators matters.

## Strengths (do not regress)

- **Manual procedure scaffolding is excellent and consistent** — every section uses Task / User / When / Steps / Expected / Common problem + a troubleshooting table + "Related sections." Directly satisfies the who/when/prereqs/expected/what-if test.
- **Raster-image alt text is genuinely descriptive** (e.g. "CSEntry first-launch Terms of Service screen with an Accept button"). Only the SVG diagrams fall short (M2).
- **Voice & audience pitch are spot-on** — active, second person, present tense; plain reassuring tone for a non-technical field audience.
- **Accessibility scaffolding is site-wide** — skip-links, `aria-label` nav/TOC, `aria-hidden` decorative SVGs, reduced-motion; tables use `<th>`; link text is descriptive. (Lighthouse: A11y + Best-Practices 100 everywhere.)
- **The sync endpoint and update guidance are stated identically everywhere** — `https://csweb.asiansocial.org/csweb/api`; "remove + re-add; the ⋮ shortcut can miss versions." (This is the consistency that the R6 alignment pass established — H1 is the one remaining gap.)
