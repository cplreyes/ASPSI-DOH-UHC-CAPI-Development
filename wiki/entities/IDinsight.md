# IDinsight

International data-and-evidence advisory firm. **The Year 1 implementer of the DOH UHC Survey**, predecessor to [[wiki/entities/ASPSI|ASPSI]] for [[wiki/concepts/UHC Survey Year 2|Year 2]]. Offices in Dakar, Lusaka, Manila, Nairobi, New Delhi, Rabat, and Remote.

## Role in the UHC Survey Project

- **Year 1 (2024)**: Designed and conducted the first round of the UHC Survey under commission from [[wiki/entities/DOH-PMSMD|DOH-PMSMD]]. Covered 70 active UHCIS, 1,135 facilities, 11,582 healthcare workers, 7,824 patients, and 31,674 household members. See [[wiki/sources/Source - IDinsight UHC Survey 2024 Final Report]].
- **Year 2 (2025–2026)**: No longer the implementer. ASPSI was awarded the Year 2 contract. IDinsight remains a knowledge source and is consulted occasionally — they participated in the **Dec 1 kick-off meeting** with DOH and ASPSI to share Year 1 findings, and **Mr. Alec Lim** has been the named DOH-side IDinsight contact in Year 2 communications (referenced in clarification meetings on methodology, infirmaries, outpatient variables — Jan 15, 2026).

## Why IDinsight matters to Carl

Three reasons:

1. **Stack continuity break** — Year 1 used **SurveyCTO**, not [[wiki/concepts/CSPro|CSPro]]. There are no usable CAPI artefacts to inherit; Carl is rebuilding from scratch on a new stack. This is a deliberate design choice for Year 2, not an oversight.
2. **Indicator continuity requirement** — Year 2 must produce indicators commensurable with Year 1 so DOH can track change over time. The Year 1 instruments and indicator definitions are the de facto specification Carl's CSPro forms must match in semantic content (even though the encoding differs).
3. **Reference point for clarifications** — When Year 2 design questions arise about how Year 1 handled something (e.g., the Jan 15 2026 clarification meeting on infirmaries and outpatient variables), Alec Lim is the authoritative answer.
