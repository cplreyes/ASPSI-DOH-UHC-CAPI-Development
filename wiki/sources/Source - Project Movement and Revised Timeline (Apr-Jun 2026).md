## The June 19 timeline cascade (Myra → DOH — the governing projection)

DOH asked Myra (Jun 19) for the estimated timeline impact if the household tool + clarifications require further revision. Her reply quantifies why the team is **holding** the June comments (parked decision):

> Adopting the June-10 comments now triggers a **3-pathway cascade**:
> 1. **SJREB** — ethics approval must be on the same tool version PSA reviews; revised tools ⇒ re-submit / wait ~2 weeks for ethics re-approval, or file an amendment (duration unknown).
> 2. **CAPI** — "Our developer estimates **4 weeks** before we have close-to-bug-free CAPI versions" (after 4–5 weeks already spent testing/debugging the 4 tools).
> 3. **PSA** — revised + translated + CAPI re-submission **restarts the 20-working-day PSA review clock** (starts only when the completeness checklist is met).

**Net impact if revised now:**
- **Pre-testing pushed back 8–10 weeks** (its precondition is ethics approval + updated CAPI); pretest itself takes ~2 weeks.
- **Deliverable 2 completable only by end of September at the earliest** ⇒ knock-on to field-supervisor + enumerator training and data-collection rollout.
- **Data collection best case: November 2026.** Myra (Jun 16): *"the December 2026 deadline will definitely not be met."*

**Holding the comments keeps Deliverable 2 on track for end of July.** Rationale (per the TOR): ASPSI must anyway submit a report consolidating ALL tool changes from SJREB + PSA reviews + pretest results, so the efficient path is to **park the June comments and revise the tools (and then the CAPI) once**, after the reviews/pretest — not in a separate mid-review round.
