# Source — Annex G: Matrix of DOH Recommendations and ASPSI Response

4-page matrix accompanying the Apr 20 2026 Revised Inception Report submission. Documents **23 remarks** from three DOH-side origins — PMSMD, ADB Consultant (Ms. Xylee Javier), and the **DOH 11th EXECOM Meeting (September 2024)** — paired with ASPSI's response for each. This is the **change-rationale map** for all four survey instruments (F1/F2/F3/F4) between Year 1 and Year 2.

## Per-instrument crosswalk

### F1 — Facility Head

- **Added** (new coverage vs. Yr1): #1 BUCAS / PuroKalusugan / ZBB module (Section E already covered BUCAS & GAMOT; expect expansion), #3 EMR-use-in-decision-making items, #22 BUCAS-bypass indicator context.
- **Sampling frame** (affects F1 UNIVERSE, not schema): #14 public+private primary-care + hospitals via NHFR; #17 not all Level 3 hospitals in sample; #21 107 UHC IS + 17 non-UHC sites (124 total).
- **Schema impact:** Section E (Expanded Health Programs) likely grew; Section C (UHC Implementation) likely grew EMR-utilization probes.

### F2 — Healthcare Worker

- **Deleted / reduced** (schema contraction): #8 + #16 — possible deletion of some HCW satisfaction items; #23 — burnout emphasis **reduced or removed altogether**.
- **Added:** #1 BUCAS / PuroKalusugan / ZBB items.
- **Schema impact:** expect net-neutral-to-contraction on item count; satisfaction + burnout sections shrank while UHC-programme items grew. Reconciles with Apr 20 F2 PDF being **+39 KB** (possibly same items with fuller text rather than raw adds).

### F3 — Patient

- **Added:** #1 BUCAS/PuroKalusugan/ZBB, #4 type of PhilHealth registration, #10 extra OOP payment lines (direct-to-doctor, off-facility meds/procedures, take-home meds), #11 CCT/4Ps membership.
- **Sampling:** #15 include private clinics within hospital premises during exit sampling (may affect F3 sampling concept, not schema).
- **Schema impact:** new items in Section I/J (Financial Risk Protection) and consent/PhilHealth block.

### F4 — Household (NEW STRUCTURAL CHANGE)

- **Major structural addition:** #6 + #13 establishes F4 as a **community/household survey** (not a patient-centric follow-up). Interval sampling starting from patient HH → neighbor HHs.
- **Added:** #1 BUCAS/PuroKalusugan/ZBB; #4 type of PhilHealth registration; #10 extra OOP lines; #11 WHO household expenditures module + CCT/4Ps; #12 health-seeking / delay / forgone / distress financing — **at household level regardless of facility visit**.
- **Schema impact:** F4 most likely source of largest net-item churn; household-expenditures module (Section N per index.md) may have been expanded.

## Key themes

### "PuroKalusugan" / BUCAS / ZBB drive coverage across all four instruments
Item #1 is the most-cited remark (#1 referenced by #7, #19). All four instruments now carry these items in some form.

### Community survey is the most material structural change
Items #6, #13 explicitly establish F4 as a full community/household survey (the TOR only required Facility Head + HCW + Patient). This is a **scope addition**, not a scope modification — and it's justified in-document as bias mitigation for the Yr1 patient-centric approach.

### Burnout emphasis trending toward deletion
Item #23 is the strongest "remove" signal. Combined with #8 (drop some HCW satisfaction items), the F2 direction of travel is *schema contraction*, not expansion.

### Data-presentation conventions (Item #18) affect analysis, not CAPI schema
- **Wealth quintiles**, not quartiles (aligns with PSA).
- Age groups **Under 5** and **60 and over**.
- Insurance: **Any** / **PhilHealth** / **HMO-private** (three-way).
- Facility types: **by hospital level**, plus primary-care providers including private clinics.

These will flow through to F4 household-roster age items and analysis/tabulation specs — not F1/F2/F3 CAPI items directly.

### Sampling frame: 124 sites
Items #14, #17, #21 converge on **107 UHC IS + 17 non-UHC = 124 sites**, drawn from NHFR covering Level 1–3 private + government + RHUs/health centers.
